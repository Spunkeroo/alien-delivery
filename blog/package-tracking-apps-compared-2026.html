Best Package Tracking Apps 2026: Track Every Delivery in One Place

Share on X Facebook LinkedIn

If you order anything online in 2026, you are juggling tracking numbers from USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL, Amazon, and dozens of other carriers. Checking each carrier's website individually is tedious and time-consuming. Package tracking apps solve this by consolidating all your deliveries into a single dashboard with real-time notifications, estimated delivery dates, and delivery history.

This guide compares the best package tracking apps available in 2026 across iOS, Android, and web platforms. We evaluate each app on carrier support, ease of use, notification reliability, pricing, and unique features to help you find the tracker that fits your needs.

1. 17Track -- Best Overall Free Tracker

17Track is the most comprehensive package tracking app available, supporting over 2,200 carriers across more than 220 countries. The app is completely free with no tracking limits, making it the go-to choice for frequent online shoppers, especially those who order from international sellers on platforms like AliExpress, Temu, and Wish.

Key Features

  • Carrier support: 2,200+ carriers worldwide including USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL, China Post, Royal Mail, Japan Post, and virtually every postal service and courier on the planet.
  • Auto-detection: Paste any tracking number and 17Track automatically identifies the carrier. No need to know which service is handling your package.
  • Batch tracking: Add multiple tracking numbers at once, which is particularly useful for resellers and small business owners.
  • Push notifications: Real-time alerts when your package status changes, arrives at customs, or is delivered.
  • Web, iOS, and Android: Available across all platforms with synced tracking data.

Pros and Cons

17Track's unmatched carrier support and zero cost make it the best overall tracking app. It excels at tracking international packages that move through multiple carriers and postal systems. The interface is functional though not the most visually polished. The main drawback is that tracking data for some obscure international carriers can be delayed compared to checking the carrier's website directly. Ads are present in the free version but are not intrusive.

2. Shop by Shopify -- Best for Automatic Tracking

The Shop app, built by Shopify, takes a different approach to package tracking. Instead of manually entering tracking numbers, Shop connects to your email and automatically detects order confirmations and tracking numbers from any Shopify-powered store and many other online retailers. In 2026, Shop has expanded its email parsing to cover the vast majority of online retailers, including Amazon, Walmart, and Target.

Key Features

  • Automatic email import: Connect your Gmail, Outlook, or Yahoo email account and Shop automatically finds and imports tracking numbers from order confirmation emails.
  • Live map tracking: For supported carriers, Shop displays the real-time location of your delivery driver on a map as they approach your address.
  • Carbon-neutral shipping: Shop calculates the carbon footprint of your deliveries and offsets it through verified carbon removal projects.
  • Shop Pay integration: If you use Shop Pay for online purchases, all orders are automatically tracked with one-tap checkout for future purchases.
  • Delivery notifications: Accurate push notifications for shipping, out-for-delivery, and delivered statuses.

Pros and Cons

Shop is the most user-friendly tracking app available. The automatic email import means you never have to manually copy and paste a tracking number, which alone makes it worth installing. The live map feature is genuinely useful for knowing exactly when your delivery will arrive. The main limitation is that Shop works best with Shopify stores and major US retailers. International carrier support is more limited than 17Track. Privacy-conscious users may be uncomfortable granting email access.

3. Parcel -- Best for Apple Users

Parcel is a beautifully designed tracking app built specifically for the Apple ecosystem. It offers a native iOS app, Apple Watch complication, Mac app, and deep integration with iOS features like widgets, Siri, and iCloud sync. For users invested in Apple hardware, Parcel provides the most seamless tracking experience available.

Key Features

  • Apple Watch support: View delivery status and receive notifications directly on your wrist. The complication shows a countdown to your next delivery.
  • iOS widgets: Home screen and lock screen widgets display your active deliveries at a glance.
  • Siri integration: Ask Siri about your packages and get instant status updates.
  • Carrier support: Supports over 300 carriers including all major US and international services.
  • iCloud sync: Tracking data syncs across iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple Watch automatically.

Pricing

Parcel offers a free tier that tracks up to 3 packages simultaneously. Parcel Premium unlocks unlimited tracking, priority notifications, and additional features for $4.99 per year or a one-time purchase of $9.99. This is excellent value for frequent shoppers.

4. AfterShip -- Best for Small Businesses

AfterShip is primarily known as a business-facing shipment tracking platform used by over 100,000 online retailers. However, its consumer app provides a polished tracking experience with some unique features that leverage AfterShip's extensive carrier network and tracking infrastructure.

Key Features

  • Carrier support: Over 1,100 carriers worldwide with reliable, fast tracking updates.
  • Predicted delivery date: AfterShip uses machine learning to predict delivery dates more accurately than carrier estimates, based on historical data from millions of shipments.
  • Email import: Automatically imports tracking numbers from connected email accounts.
  • Dashboard analytics: For business users, AfterShip provides delivery performance analytics, exception alerts, and customer notification tools.

Pros and Cons

AfterShip's predicted delivery dates are noticeably more accurate than standard carrier estimates, especially for international shipments that pass through multiple carriers. The business features make it the best choice for anyone running an e-commerce store who needs to track outbound shipments. For casual personal use, 17Track or Shop may be simpler and more appropriate.

5. USPS Informed Delivery -- Best for Mail Tracking

USPS Informed Delivery is a free service from the United States Postal Service that does something no other app can: it shows you scanned images of your incoming mail before it arrives. In addition to package tracking, you get a daily email or app notification showing grayscale images of every letter and flat envelope headed to your address that day.

Key Features

  • Mail images: See digital scans of incoming letters and envelopes before they arrive in your mailbox. This is unique to USPS Informed Delivery.
  • Package tracking: Track all USPS packages with real-time status updates and delivery notifications.
  • Daily digest: Receive a morning email summarizing what mail and packages are coming that day.
  • Delivery instructions: Leave delivery instructions for your letter carrier directly through the app.
  • Free: Completely free for all US residential addresses.

Limitations

Informed Delivery only tracks USPS mail and packages. It does not support UPS, FedEx, DHL, or any other carrier. The mail scanning feature is available at most but not all US addresses. Despite these limitations, the ability to see your incoming mail before it arrives is a genuinely useful feature that no other service replicates.

6. PackageRadar -- Best for International Shoppers

PackageRadar supports over 750 carriers and specializes in tracking international packages, particularly shipments from China to the US and Europe. The app provides detailed tracking through every stage of international shipping, including warehouse processing, customs clearance, and handoff between international and domestic carriers.

Key Features

  • Detailed international tracking: Shows granular status updates through every stage of cross-border shipping.
  • Customs alerts: Notifications when your package enters customs, clears customs, or encounters customs issues.
  • Historical shipping data: View delivery time averages for specific routes and carriers to set realistic expectations.
  • Free with premium option: Basic tracking is free. Premium removes ads and adds priority notifications for $2.99 per month.

Key Features to Look For in a Tracking App

Push Notifications

Reliable push notifications are the most important feature. A tracking app that does not alert you promptly when your package ships, arrives at your local post office, or is delivered defeats the purpose. Test notifications during your first few tracked packages and switch apps if alerts are delayed or missing.

Carrier Auto-Detection

The best tracking apps identify the carrier automatically when you paste a tracking number. This saves time and eliminates errors. All apps in this guide support auto-detection for major carriers, but 17Track handles the widest range of international carriers.

Email Integration

Apps that connect to your email and automatically import tracking numbers are significantly more convenient than manual entry. Shop and AfterShip both offer this feature. The trade-off is granting email access to a third-party app, which some users may prefer to avoid.

Delivery Map

Real-time delivery maps showing your driver's location are useful for timing your availability. Shop offers the best live map feature among consumer tracking apps. This feature is primarily available for last-mile delivery and is not available during transit stages.

Package Tracking Tips

Save Tracking Numbers Immediately

Add tracking numbers to your preferred app as soon as you receive the shipping confirmation email. Early tracking entries often capture pre-shipment scans and provide earlier delivery estimates. Do not wait until you are wondering where your package is.

Understand Common Tracking Statuses

"Label created" means the seller has printed a shipping label but the carrier has not yet received the package. This can last 1 to 5 days and is normal. "In transit" means the package is moving through the carrier's network. "Out for delivery" means it is on the truck and will arrive that day. "Delivery attempted" means the driver tried to deliver but could not, usually because no one was home to sign.

Use USPS Informed Delivery Even If You Use Other Apps

Informed Delivery's mail scanning feature is unique and complements any other tracking app. Knowing what checks, letters, and envelopes are arriving helps you stay on top of important mail. Sign up at informeddelivery.usps.com and pair it with 17Track or Shop for package tracking.

Track International Packages with Patience

International packages, especially from China, often show no tracking updates for 7 to 14 days while in transit across the Pacific. This is normal. The package is not lost, it is simply in a container on a ship or in a customs queue where scanning does not occur. Updates will resume once the package enters the destination country's postal system.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best all-in-one package tracking app in 2026?

17Track is the best all-in-one package tracking app in 2026 for users who order from international sellers. It supports over 2,200 carriers worldwide and is completely free. For US-focused tracking, the Shop app by Shopify is the most polished option, automatically detecting tracking numbers from your email and providing real-time push notifications. Parcel is the best choice for Apple ecosystem users with a clean interface and Apple Watch support.

Can I track all my packages in one app?

Yes. Multi-carrier tracking apps like 17Track, Parcel, and AfterShip allow you to enter tracking numbers from USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL, Amazon, and hundreds of other carriers in a single interface. Some apps also connect to your email to automatically detect and import tracking numbers from order confirmation emails, eliminating the need to manually enter each number.

Is there a free package tracking app?

Yes, several excellent package tracking apps are completely free. 17Track is free with no tracking limits and supports over 2,200 carriers. The Shop app by Shopify is free with automatic email tracking detection. USPS Informed Delivery is free for USPS packages and also shows scanned images of incoming mail. Google Search also provides package tracking when you search a tracking number directly.

How do package tracking apps work?

Package tracking apps work by querying carrier APIs and tracking databases using your tracking number. When you enter a tracking number, the app identifies the carrier, sends a request to that carrier's system, and retrieves the current status and scan history of your package. The app then normalizes this data into a consistent format and sends you push notifications when the status changes. Some apps also use email parsing to automatically detect tracking numbers from your order confirmations.

Why does my tracking number show no information?

A tracking number that shows no information usually means the package has not yet been scanned into the carrier's system. This is normal and typically resolves within 24 to 48 hours after the seller generates the shipping label. Other causes include an incorrect tracking number, the carrier's system experiencing delays in updating, or the package being shipped with a carrier that your tracking app does not support. If tracking shows no updates for more than 5 business days, contact the seller or carrier directly.
