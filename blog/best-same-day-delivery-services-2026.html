Best Same-Day Delivery Services Compared 2026

In 2026, the major retailers and delivery platforms have expanded their same-day networks to cover most urban and suburban areas, competing fiercely on speed, cost, and product selection. Whether you need groceries in an hour, electronics by dinner, or household essentials before bedtime, there is a service designed for your situation.

This guide provides a thorough, unbiased comparison of every major same-day delivery service available in the United States in 2026. Amazon Same-Day Delivery

Overview

Amazon remains the largest and most comprehensive same-day delivery service in the United States. With fulfillment centers strategically placed across more than 120 metropolitan areas, Amazon can deliver millions of items on the same day you order them. The service is available to Amazon Prime members, and in 2026 Amazon has continued to expand both the geographic footprint and the catalog of same-day eligible products.

Speed and Availability

Amazon same-day delivery windows typically range from 5 to 12 hours depending on when you place the order and your distance from the nearest fulfillment center. In major cities like New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Dallas, morning orders often arrive by evening the same day. Amazon has also expanded its ultra-fast delivery hub network, offering delivery in as little as 3 to 4 hours on select items in densely populated urban cores.

Cost Structure

For Prime members, same-day delivery is free on qualifying orders of $35 or more. Orders below $35 carry a $2.99 delivery fee. Non-Prime members pay $9.99 to $12.99 per same-day delivery. Amazon Prime costs $14.99 per month or $139 per year, making the per-delivery cost effectively zero for frequent shoppers who would subscribe to Prime regardless.

Product Selection

Amazon's same-day catalog includes millions of items across categories: electronics, household goods, personal care, office supplies, books, toys, pet supplies, and an expanding grocery selection through Amazon Fresh and Whole Foods Market integration. The breadth of the same-day catalog is unmatched by any competitor. However, not every Amazon item qualifies for same-day delivery. Look for the "Get it today" badge on product listings to confirm eligibility.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Largest product catalog, fastest speeds in metro areas, free for Prime members on orders over $35, reliable tracking and notifications, easy returns.
  • Cons: Requires Prime membership for best pricing, $35 minimum for free same-day, availability varies by zip code, groceries require separate Amazon Fresh or Whole Foods ordering.

Walmart Same-Day Delivery

Overview

Walmart has invested billions of dollars in its delivery infrastructure and in 2026 offers same-day delivery from over 4,700 stores across the United States. Walmart's advantage lies in using its massive store network as fulfillment hubs, which means delivery distances are shorter and speeds are faster than shipping from a centralized warehouse. For groceries and household essentials, Walmart is a direct competitor to Amazon and often wins on pricing.

Speed and Availability

Walmart offers standard same-day delivery within a 3 to 5 hour window and Walmart Express delivery in under 2 hours for an additional fee. In 2026, Walmart Express is available in over 3,800 locations covering approximately 80 percent of the US population. Delivery slots are available from 8 AM to 8 PM in most markets, with some locations extending to 10 PM.

Cost Structure

Walmart Plus members ($12.95 per month or $98 per year) receive free same-day delivery on orders of $35 or more. Express delivery adds a $10 fee. Without Walmart Plus, same-day delivery costs $7.95 to $9.95 per order. Walmart does not mark up grocery prices for delivery, meaning the items cost the same online as they do in-store. This is a significant advantage over services that add a 15 to 20 percent price markup on delivered items.

Product Selection

Walmart same-day delivery covers groceries, household essentials, electronics, clothing, toys, pharmacy items, and general merchandise. The selection mirrors what is available at your local Walmart store, which means it varies by location but is generally extensive. Grocery delivery is Walmart's strongest category, with fresh produce, meat, dairy, bakery items, and frozen foods all available.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: No price markup on groceries, Walmart Plus is cheaper than Prime, massive store network means broad coverage, Express delivery under 2 hours, strong grocery selection.
  • Cons: Product selection limited to local store inventory, Express delivery carries extra $10 fee, general merchandise selection smaller than Amazon, delivery quality varies by market.

Target Same-Day Delivery (Shipt)

Overview

Target's same-day delivery operates through Shipt, the personal shopping service that Target acquired in 2017. Shipt shoppers pick items from your local Target store and deliver them to your door, typically within 1 to 3 hours. The service covers over 5,000 Target locations and extends to other retailers in the Shipt network.

Speed and Availability

Shipt delivery windows are 1 to 3 hours from order placement. You can also schedule deliveries for specific time slots later in the day. Shipt shoppers communicate with you during the shopping process, sending photos and asking about substitutions in real time. This personal touch is a differentiator that automated delivery systems cannot match.

Cost Structure

A Shipt membership costs $10.99 per month or $99 per year, providing free delivery on Target orders over $35. Without a membership, individual delivery orders cost $10.99 each. Target Circle 360, the retailer's premium loyalty tier at $49 per year, also includes Shipt delivery benefits. Note that Shipt applies a small service fee on each order, typically 5 to 10 percent of the order total, which is charged even for members.

Product Selection

Target same-day delivery covers the full range of Target's product catalog: groceries, clothing, beauty products, home decor, electronics, baby products, pet supplies, and seasonal items. Target's curated private-label brands like Good & Gather (groceries), All in Motion (activewear), and Threshold (home furnishings) are all available for same-day delivery. The selection is narrower than Amazon or Walmart but the quality of Target's private labels is a draw for many shoppers.

Instacart Same-Day Delivery

Overview

Instacart is the leading third-party grocery delivery platform, partnering with over 1,500 retail banners including Costco, Kroger, Publix, Safeway, Sprouts, Aldi, and hundreds of regional grocery chains. Unlike Amazon, Walmart, and Target, which deliver from their own stores, Instacart connects you to virtually every major grocery retailer through a single app.

Speed and Availability

Standard Instacart delivery takes 1 to 3 hours. Priority delivery, available for an additional $2 to $3 fee, targets a 30-minute to 1-hour window. Instacart operates in over 14,000 cities across the US and Canada, giving it the broadest geographic grocery delivery coverage. Delivery hours vary by retailer but typically run from 8 AM to 9 PM.

Cost Structure

Instacart Plus costs $9.99 per month or $99 per year, providing free delivery on orders over $35 and reduced service fees. Without a subscription, delivery fees range from $3.99 to $7.99 per order. Service fees of 5 percent apply to all orders. Instacart item prices may include markups of 5 to 15 percent above in-store prices, depending on the retailer. Some retailers, like Costco and Aldi, maintain parity pricing on Instacart.

Unique Advantage

Instacart's greatest strength is retailer variety. If you prefer Kroger for groceries, Costco for bulk items, and Sprouts for organic produce, Instacart lets you order from all three through one platform. No other service offers this level of retailer aggregation. The personal shopper model also means you can communicate preferences and substitutions in real time.

Gopuff Instant Delivery

Overview

Gopuff operates differently from every other service on this list. Instead of picking items from retail stores, Gopuff ships from its own network of micro-fulfillment centers stocked with convenience items, snacks, beverages, household essentials, over-the-counter medications, and alcohol (where permitted). The focus is on speed: delivery in under 30 minutes.

Speed and Availability

Gopuff's average delivery time is 15 to 30 minutes, making it the fastest option for immediate needs. The service operates in over 650 cities across the US as of 2026. Hours vary but many markets offer late-night delivery until 2 or 3 AM, which is a major advantage over traditional retailers.

Cost Structure

Gopuff charges a flat $3.95 delivery fee per order. Gopuff Fam membership costs $7.95 per month for free delivery on orders over $15. Item prices are comparable to convenience store pricing, meaning they are higher than grocery store prices but competitive with what you would pay at a 7-Eleven or CVS. Gopuff is not a grocery replacement but rather a convenience store replacement with the added benefit of home delivery.

Costco Same-Day Delivery

Overview

Costco offers same-day delivery through a partnership with Instacart. Costco members can order groceries, household items, and select bulk merchandise for same-day delivery. The service is available to all Costco members (Executive and Gold Star) and does not require a separate Instacart subscription, though Instacart Plus members receive additional benefits.

Cost and Pricing

Costco same-day delivery requires a Costco membership ($65 or $130 per year). Delivery fees start at $3.99 for orders over $35. Instacart Plus members get free delivery on Costco orders over $35. Items are priced at or near Costco in-store prices, with some items carrying a small markup. The combination of Costco's bulk pricing and same-day delivery makes this an attractive option for families and households that shop Costco regularly.

The True Cost of Same-Day Delivery

The advertised delivery fee is never the complete picture. Same-day delivery costs include the delivery fee itself (waived with subscriptions on orders over $35), service fees that range from 5 to 15 percent of the order total, item markups that add 5 to 20 percent over in-store prices on some platforms, driver tips (industry standard is 15 to 20 percent or at least $5), and surge pricing during peak demand periods like holidays and severe weather events.

On a $50 grocery order, actual costs break down roughly as follows. With Walmart Plus there is no delivery fee, no item markup, a small bag fee in some areas, and a tip, totaling approximately $55 to $60. With Instacart Plus there is no delivery fee, a 5 percent service fee, a possible 5 to 15 percent item markup, and a tip, totaling approximately $60 to $70. Without any subscription, the same $50 order could cost $70 to $80 after all fees and tips.

The No-Markup Advantage

Walmart is one of the only major services that guarantees no item markup on grocery delivery. The milk, bread, and eggs in your cart cost exactly what they cost on the shelf. This single factor can save $5 to $15 per order compared to services that mark up item prices, which adds up to hundreds of dollars per year for regular delivery users.

How to Save on Same-Day Delivery

Stack Subscriptions Strategically

You do not need every delivery subscription. If you already pay for Amazon Prime, use Amazon same-day for general merchandise. Add Walmart Plus ($12.95 per month) for groceries at no markup. This two-subscription stack covers most delivery needs for under $28 per month combined.

Meet the Free Delivery Minimum

Every platform offers free delivery on orders above a minimum threshold, usually $35. Plan your orders to meet this threshold rather than placing multiple small orders. A $25 order with a $7.95 delivery fee costs more than a $40 order with free delivery and extra items you needed anyway.

Use Pickup When Possible

Both Walmart and Target offer free same-day pickup with no minimum order. If you pass by the store on your way home, ordering for pickup eliminates delivery fees, service fees, tips, and item markups entirely. It is the most cost-effective way to get same-day orders.

Compare Across Platforms

The same item can cost significantly different amounts across platforms due to varying item markups, delivery fees, and service fees. Before placing a large order, spend two minutes checking the total cost on Amazon, Walmart, and Instacart. The savings on a $100 order can be $10 to $20.

Tip Appropriately

Same-day delivery drivers and shoppers rely on tips as a significant portion of their income. The standard tip is 15 to 20 percent of the order total or $5 minimum, whichever is greater. Tip after delivery so you can adjust based on the quality of service. Good tippers consistently report faster acceptance of their orders and better service.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which same-day delivery service is fastest in 2026?

Amazon same-day delivery is the fastest overall, with delivery windows as short as 5 hours in major metro areas for Prime members. Walmart Express delivers in under 2 hours for grocery and household items in select markets. For groceries specifically, Instacart Priority delivery can arrive in as little as 30 minutes in dense urban areas, though the typical window is 1 to 2 hours. Gopuff is the fastest for convenience items, averaging 15 to 30 minutes.

Is same-day delivery free with Amazon Prime?

Yes, Amazon offers free same-day delivery for Prime members on qualifying orders of $35 or more in eligible zip codes. Orders under $35 incur a $2.99 delivery fee. The service covers millions of items, though availability depends on your proximity to an Amazon fulfillment center. As of 2026, Amazon same-day delivery is available in over 120 metro areas across the United States.

How much does same-day delivery cost in 2026?

Costs vary by provider. Amazon Prime same-day is free on orders over $35. Walmart offers free same-day delivery on orders over $35 with Walmart Plus membership ($12.95 per month). Target same-day via Shipt costs $10.99 per month or $99 per year for unlimited deliveries on orders over $35. Instacart charges $3.99 to $7.99 per delivery without a subscription, or free delivery on orders over $35 with Instacart Plus at $9.99 per month.

What items can I get delivered same-day?

The range of same-day eligible items has expanded significantly. Amazon offers same-day on electronics, household goods, books, beauty products, and groceries. Walmart and Target cover groceries, household essentials, electronics, clothing, and toys. Instacart focuses on groceries and household items from over 1,500 retail partners. Large or heavy items like furniture, major appliances, and oversized packages are generally excluded from same-day options.

Is Walmart Plus or Amazon Prime better for same-day delivery?

Amazon Prime is better for general merchandise same-day delivery, with a larger catalog of eligible items and broader geographic coverage. Walmart Plus is better for grocery same-day delivery, with competitive pricing and no markup on grocery items. Walmart Plus costs $12.95 per month versus Amazon Prime at $14.99 per month. If you primarily need grocery delivery, Walmart Plus offers better value. For a mix of grocery and general merchandise, Amazon Prime is more versatile.

Conclusion

The best same-day delivery service in 2026 depends on what you are ordering and how quickly you need it. Amazon dominates general merchandise with the broadest catalog and reliable same-day speeds. Walmart wins on grocery delivery value with no item markups and competitive subscription pricing. Target via Shipt excels for Target loyalists who want a personal shopping experience. Instacart offers unmatched retailer variety for grocery delivery. Gopuff is the speed champion for convenience items and late-night needs. Costco same-day through Instacart brings bulk warehouse value to your doorstep.

For most households, the optimal strategy is combining two subscriptions: Amazon Prime for general merchandise and either Walmart Plus or Instacart Plus for groceries, depending on whether you prioritize no-markup pricing or multi-retailer access. The total cost of two subscriptions pays for itself within a few orders per month through waived delivery fees alone.
