The True Cost of Food Delivery

Before comparing individual apps, it is important to understand the layered fee structure that all delivery platforms use. The advertised "low delivery fee" is never the total cost. A typical food delivery order includes the menu price (often 15-30% higher than in-store), a delivery fee ($1-8), a service fee (10-18% of order subtotal), a small order fee ($2-3 on orders under $10-15), an optional tip (industry standard is 15-20% or $3-5 minimum), and applicable taxes.

On a $20 food order, total fees (before tip) typically add $7-12, bringing the actual cost to $27-32. With a standard tip, you are paying $32-37 for $20 worth of food. Understanding this math is essential for making informed decisions about when delivery makes sense and which app minimizes the markup.

DoorDash

Overview

DoorDash is the dominant food delivery platform in the United States, commanding approximately 67% market share in 2026. This market position gives DoorDash the largest restaurant selection in most cities and towns, including many smaller and suburban areas where competitors have limited coverage.

Key Features

  • Restaurant network: 390,000+ partner restaurants across all 50 states, the most comprehensive coverage of any platform.
  • DashPass subscription: $9.99/month for $0 delivery fees and reduced service fees on orders of $12+ from eligible restaurants. Approximately 80% of restaurants are DashPass-eligible.
  • Delivery speed: Average 25-35 minutes in urban areas. DoorDash's "Priority Delivery" option ($1-3 extra) moves your order to the front of the queue.
  • Grocery delivery: Expanded significantly, now partnering with major grocery chains for same-day delivery.
  • Pickup option: Order through the app for pickup to avoid all delivery and service fees while still earning DashPass credits.

Pros and Cons

DoorDash's greatest advantage is coverage. If you live outside a major metro area, DoorDash likely has significantly more restaurant options than competitors. DashPass provides genuine savings for users who order 3+ times per month. The app interface is clean and reliable, and delivery tracking is accurate. The main drawbacks are that DoorDash's service fees can be higher than competitors for non-DashPass users, and menu price markups are sometimes more aggressive than on other platforms.

Uber Eats

Overview

Uber Eats holds approximately 23% of the US food delivery market and benefits from integration with Uber's ride-sharing ecosystem. The platform offers a strong restaurant selection in urban areas and has expanded aggressively into grocery and convenience store delivery.

Key Features

  • Restaurant network: 350,000+ partner restaurants, strongest in major metro areas.
  • Uber One subscription: $9.99/month for $0 delivery fees on orders of $15+, 5% discount on eligible orders, and benefits that extend to Uber rides (10% off rides, priority pickup).
  • Delivery speed: Average 25-40 minutes. Uber's driver network from ride-sharing provides strong delivery capacity during off-peak hours.
  • Multi-stop delivery: Order from multiple restaurants in a single delivery for a combined fee.
  • Grocery and alcohol: Extensive grocery delivery and alcohol delivery (where legally permitted) through the same app.

Pros and Cons

Uber Eats excels for users who also use Uber for transportation, as Uber One covers both services. The multi-stop feature is genuinely useful for households where different people want food from different restaurants. Urban coverage is excellent. Drawbacks include weaker coverage in suburban and rural areas compared to DoorDash, occasionally longer delivery times during peak hours when drivers are split between rides and deliveries, and the $15 minimum for Uber One free delivery (higher than DashPass's $12).

Grubhub

Overview

Grubhub, now owned by Wonder Group, holds approximately 8% of the US market. Despite being smaller, Grubhub maintains strong relationships with independent restaurants and often has partnerships with local favorites that are not available on larger platforms.

Key Features

  • Restaurant network: 150,000+ partner restaurants, with particular strength in independent and local restaurants.
  • Grubhub+ subscription: $9.99/month for $0 delivery fees on orders of $12+, and included free for Amazon Prime members as of 2024.
  • Loyalty perks: Grubhub's reward system offers credits toward free food based on order history.
  • Corporate accounts: Strong corporate ordering and catering features make Grubhub a popular choice for business meals.
  • Campus dining: Partnerships with universities for campus food delivery.

Pros and Cons

Grubhub's biggest advantage in 2026 is the free Grubhub+ inclusion with Amazon Prime, which effectively gives Prime members free delivery on all qualifying orders without an additional subscription cost. The independent restaurant focus means you may find local gems on Grubhub that are absent from DoorDash or Uber Eats. Drawbacks include a smaller restaurant network overall, less geographic coverage, and an app experience that is functional but not as polished as competitors.

Instacart

Overview

Instacart is primarily known as a grocery delivery service, but has expanded into restaurant and prepared food delivery in 2026. Its strength lies in grocery delivery from a vast network of retail partners, with restaurant delivery as a growing but still secondary feature.

Key Features

  • Grocery network: Partners with over 1,500 retail banners including Costco, Kroger, Publix, Safeway, Sprouts, and hundreds of regional chains.
  • Instacart+ subscription: $9.99/month or $99/year for $0 delivery fees on orders of $35+, reduced service fees, and free pickup on all orders.
  • Restaurant delivery: Available in select markets through partnerships with local restaurants and prepared food retailers.
  • Same-day delivery: Typical delivery windows of 1-2 hours for grocery orders, with an "Priority" option for faster service.
  • Personal shoppers: Instacart uses personal shoppers who communicate with you during the shopping process, allowing real-time substitutions.

Pros and Cons

Instacart is the clear leader for grocery delivery and the best choice if your primary need is getting groceries delivered rather than restaurant meals. The personal shopper model provides a level of communication and flexibility that automated systems cannot match. Drawbacks include higher order minimums for free delivery ($35 vs. $12-15 for restaurant apps), limited restaurant delivery coverage, and the fact that Instacart grocery prices sometimes include markups above in-store pricing.

Subscription Plans: Are They Worth It?

All four platforms offer subscription plans at roughly $9.99/month. Whether a subscription is worth it depends entirely on your order frequency.

A typical delivery fee without a subscription is $3-6. If you order twice per month, you save $6-12 in delivery fees, roughly breaking even on the $9.99 subscription. At three or more orders per month, the subscription pays for itself clearly. For very frequent users (8+ orders/month), the savings can exceed $40/month.

The Amazon Prime Hack

If you already have Amazon Prime ($14.99/month), you get Grubhub+ included at no additional cost. This makes Grubhub effectively the cheapest option for Prime members, since the subscription cost is zero. Even if Grubhub has fewer restaurants in your area, it is worth checking first for any order since you are already paying for the benefit.

Tips for Saving Money on Food Delivery

Compare Across Apps

The same restaurant can have different prices, fees, and promotions on different platforms. Spending 60 seconds checking two or three apps before ordering can save $3-8 per order. Apps like FoodBoss automate this comparison.

Order for Pickup

Ordering through a delivery app for pickup eliminates delivery fees, often reduces service fees, and lets you avoid tipping for delivery. If the restaurant is within a short drive, pickup saves 30-50% compared to delivery on the same order.

Increase Your Order Size

Fees are largely fixed regardless of order size. A $15 order with $8 in fees represents a 53% markup. A $40 order with $8 in fees is only a 20% markup. Order for multiple meals, cook once and eat leftovers, or combine orders with family members or roommates to spread fixed costs across more food.

Take Advantage of Promotions

All platforms regularly offer promotional discounts, especially for new users. Check for promotions before every order -- many users leave money on the table by ordering out of habit without checking for active deals. Sign up for email notifications from all four platforms.

Use Cashback Apps

Cashback apps like Rakuten and TopCashback frequently offer 2-8% cashback on food delivery orders placed through their links. This stacks on top of any other savings and adds up for regular delivery users.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which food delivery app is cheapest in 2026?

For individual orders without a subscription, Grubhub tends to have the lowest fees on average, though this varies by restaurant and location. For frequent users, DoorDash's DashPass ($9.99/month) and Uber Eats' Uber One ($9.99/month) both eliminate delivery fees on qualifying orders, making them the cheapest options for regular users who order 3 or more times per month.

Is DashPass or Uber One a better value?

Both cost $9.99/month and eliminate delivery fees on qualifying orders. DashPass is the better value if you primarily order food delivery, as DoorDash has the largest restaurant network. Uber One is better if you also use Uber rides, as the subscription covers both services. Uber One also includes grocery delivery savings through Uber Eats' expanded grocery partnerships. The break-even point for both is approximately 2-3 orders per month.

Why are food delivery apps so expensive?

The high cost of food delivery comes from multiple fee layers: delivery fees ($1-8), service fees (10-18% of order), small order fees (on orders under $10-15), and often inflated menu prices (restaurants frequently charge 15-30% more on delivery apps than in-store). Tips add further cost. A $15 restaurant meal can easily cost $25-30 delivered. Subscription plans and promotional offers are the most effective ways to reduce these costs.

Which food delivery app has the most restaurants?

DoorDash has the largest restaurant network in the United States, with over 390,000 partner restaurants across all 50 states. Uber Eats is second with approximately 350,000 restaurants. Grubhub has around 150,000. However, restaurant availability varies significantly by location -- in some cities, Uber Eats or Grubhub may have better coverage than DoorDash for specific cuisines or neighborhoods.

Can you use multiple food delivery apps to save money?

Yes, and this is one of the best strategies for saving money. Different apps offer different promotions, and the same restaurant can have different prices and fees across platforms. Before ordering, check all available apps to compare total costs including delivery fees, service fees, and any active promotions. Apps like FoodBoss aggregate prices across platforms to show you the cheapest option for each order.

Conclusion
